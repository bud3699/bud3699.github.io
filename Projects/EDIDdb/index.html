About the Project

EDID-DB was created to catalog Extended Display Identification Data (EDID) blocks from displays around the world. It became one of the largest archives of unique EDIDs, enabling developers, repair technicians, and hardware enthusiasts to better understand and troubleshoot display devices.

The platform supported searching, uploading, fingerprinting, and parsing of EDIDs into human-readable fields. Users could inspect manufacturer info, product IDs, serial numbers, and more, with options to download and study each one.

A heartfelt thank you goes out to everyone who donated and supported the project. Your contributions helped keep the database running and made it possible to grow into the large resource it became.

Unfortunately, EDID-DB is no longer available. Even though I was the sole programmer behind the project, it was hosted under a YouTuber’s subdomain. That individual chose to delete the entire code base and database without warning. The mindset was clear: rather than let the project continue, they preferred to remove it entirely. This was further shown by leaving only a default Apache landing page in its place — suggesting the intent was to eventually replace my work with their own. The project was cut short in this way, despite the value it was providing to the community.
